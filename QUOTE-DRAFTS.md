### Key Events Referenced

- **February 5-19, 2022:** First drop - PANIC window - first scare, many sold

- **June-October 2022:** Uncertainty - nobody knew what would happen
- **October 31 - December 3, 2022:** Another drop - PANIC window - hope lost
- **December 2022 onwards:** Long decline - just kept going down

### Analysis: Panic Sells vs Profit-Taking

**Key finding:** Of 114 early holders (before Feb 2022):
- **Pure profit-takers** (sold during runs, NOT during drops): **8 people** (7%)
- **Pure panic sellers** (sold during drops, NOT during runs): **8 people** (7%)
- **Both** (sold during runs AND during drops): **88 people** (77%)
- **Never sold**: 7 people (6%)

**Conclusion:** Almost everyone who sold did so during BOTH good times and bad times. True disciplined profit-taking (selling during runs without panic selling during drops) is extremely rare - only 8 out of 114 early holders.

This means we should NOT broadly celebrate "profit-taking" because the data shows most sellers were panic selling during crashes, not disciplined profit-taking during runs. The honest story is: "You sold some along the way - we all felt those moments of panic."

### Themes
- Acknowledge what happened without dwelling
- No judgment for selling - "we all felt that", "who wouldn't?", "totally normal"
- **Be honest about panic selling** - most selling happened during drops, not calculated profit-taking
- Euphoria = excitement going UP, not chaos
- Smooth transitions from story to community - "we've been doing our own things"
- Ask "what have you been up to?" - long-term holders are focused on other things
- Welcome language for new members - "now so are you", "glad you found us"
- Learning curve acknowledgment - "everyone goes through this", "we've all been there"
- Hope through community connection, not promises
- Diamond Grip holders - never sold, different kind of commitment
